Who am I ?

I'm Emilie, a twenty years old student in Interactive Design at Gobelins, l'école de l'image, searching for a year of work-study in webdesign, print and/or UX/UI design starting september 2020.

Design is for me a way of conveying feelings and messages. It may be because I got deeply moved by so many beautiful things, I ended up taking that path to do the same, stir emotions in others' heart, and began to learn how to do like the ones I admired. No matter how difficult it was, I tried and loved the outcome of this labor.

I try to please one's eyes. Yet, through my work, I do my best, with the current knowledge I have, to deliver an accessible and intuitive interface (with some personal craziness though) to the user so that they can take their time looking at what they were searching for in the first place.

lunar
calendar

a school individual project*
36h project

The lunar calendar I have made on Illustrator includes the rare astronomical events of 2020. It is based on the Gregorian calendar and is separated into trimesters in order to be readable.

The dates are arranged on a circle to recall the spherical trait of the planets. The jellyfish in the center — symbolizing “daughter of the Moon and the Oceans” and Sacred Feminine uniting Venus, Neptune and the Moon at the same time — mixes with blue shooting stars which recall space and its relationship with our aquatic environments, uterus of planets and life.

Each trimester begins with a note announcing the seasons, accompanied by a poem from the Ogura Hyakunin Isshu - a Japanese anthology of one hundred poems - for every season.

yearbook

school covid group project*
a five days workshop

Instructions

For the third workshop, each group had to have four members. We were to choose between doing a yearbook, a poster or a site for the 20 years of Gobelins. We had five days. My group chose the yearbook. We were tasked as a whole class of twenty-six students to crop and resize a thousand pictures from 1993 to 2021 (it isn't twenty years but that's what the instructions said).

How we worked

We only had five days for the workshop, so we had to crop and resize the pictures with Photoshop in a day. We couldn't afford to fall behind, even though it was hard to organize each other during the confinement. We did everything while communicating on Discord. The next day, we began the real work and started, with my group, to talk design and for who it was designed. We did a lot of researches, then we distributed the tasks. I did the layouts with InDesign and some illustrations with Illustrator, including the characters, another one did the other illustrations under the grid, and the others did some other layouts. We always consulted each other for everything we did. Then, we filled the layouts.

Description

We came up with an old-school, cheerful theme, recalling the computer side of the formations we were working on for this yearbook. We used grids, the 256 web colors of the 90's, fonts and illustrations recalling old computers. It was hard to design a yearbook since there isn't much inspiration on the web, but we took the challenge and were glad we did it since it made us think greatly and come up with something unique.
